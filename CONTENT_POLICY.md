This is a **public** archive. The default posture is caution: when in doubt, leave it out.
The single rule that everything else follows from is **my own words and public links only**.

## What this archive publishes

- My own summaries, notes, and reflections, written by me.
- Public, freely accessible resource links (course homepages, free textbooks, arXiv papers, public videos).
- Reading metadata: title, authors, year, venue, and a public link.
- High-level project summaries and write-ups.
- Portfolio-safe code links (cleaned side projects, rewrites, demos, or final projects where course policy allows).
- Weekly progress logs: what I focused on, what worked, and where I struggled.
- High-level, non-identifying assignment labels (e.g., "started the sequence-labeling project"), never the prompt itself.

## What this archive never publishes

- Full syllabi from Canvas.
- Paid textbook PDFs or other copyrighted full texts.
- Lecture slides, recordings, or notes that reproduce a lecture.
- Assignment prompts, rubrics, exams, quizzes, or answer keys.
- Autograder files or test harnesses.
- Canvas screenshots or professor announcements.
- Classmate content of any kind.
- Private or restricted datasets, or any personal data.
- Direct assignment solutions, unless the course explicitly permits publishing them.

## Code policy

Published code is **portfolio-safe by default**. That means cleaned side projects, from-scratch
rewrites, demos, or final projects where the course allows sharing. Raw assignment solutions are not
published unless the instructor or course policy explicitly permits it. When in doubt, the code stays
private and only a high-level write-up goes public.

## Academic integrity

This archive is a record of *my* learning, not a back channel for course materials. It is built to
respect The University of Texas at Austin's honor code and each course's individual sharing policy.
Where a course policy is stricter than this document, **the course policy wins**.

## Affiliation

This is an independent, personal project. It is not affiliated with, sponsored by, or endorsed by The
University of Texas at Austin. Course names are referenced only for personal record-keeping.

## Reporting
