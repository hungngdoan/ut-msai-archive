Most learning logs die in week four. Not because people stop learning, but because the log becomes one
more thing to maintain right when the workload peaks. I'm starting this one two months early on purpose:
if the habit is already automatic before the first deadline, it survives contact with a real semester.

There are three things I want this archive to do.

## Make my own progress legible to me

It's easy to feel busy and hard to know whether I'm actually getting better. A weekly entry that names
what clicked and what didn't forces the question. The gaps section matters more than the wins — a log
that only records victories is marketing, not learning.

## Grow a portfolio as a byproduct

I'd rather have a portfolio fall out of the work than assemble one in a panic at graduation. If each
project gets a clean, public-safe write-up as I go, the portfolio builds itself.

## Keep me honest about what's public

Putting this in public forces a discipline I want anyway: separating my own understanding from course
materials I have no right to republish. The rule is simple and I wrote it down — my own words and
public links only. Everything private stays private.

If, two years from now, this archive is sparse but honest, that's a success. If it's polished but
abandoned by October, it failed. The whole design is bent toward the first outcome.
