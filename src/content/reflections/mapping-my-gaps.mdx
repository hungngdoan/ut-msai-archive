The temptation before a hard program is to review what you already enjoy. I'd rather spend the summer
on what will actually hurt. So this is a deliberately uncomfortable inventory of where I'm weak going
into Natural Language Processing, Ethics in AI, and Deep Learning.

## Math I lean on but don't own

- **Matrix calculus.** I can follow gradient derivations; I'm slow to produce them. In Deep Learning
  that gap turns into time lost on every problem set.
- **Probability under pressure.** I know the identities. Applying Bayes' rule quickly, without
  re-deriving it, is a different skill, and I don't have it yet.

## Engineering habits that are still soft

- **Debugging training runs.** When a network won't learn, I currently guess. I want a checklist I
  trust: data, shapes, learning rate, initialization, in that order.
- **Reading papers actively.** I read papers like articles. I need to read them like code — reproduce
  the core claim at small scale instead of nodding along.

## The thing I keep avoiding

The ethics material. It's easy to treat it as the soft course and coast. That's exactly the wrong
instinct: the hardest real-world AI failures are not math errors, they're decisions about what to
optimize and who bears the cost. I want to take it as seriously as the technical courses, and writing
that down here is how I hold myself to it.

The point of naming these now is leverage. Two months of prep aimed at real weaknesses beats two months
of polishing what I can already do.
