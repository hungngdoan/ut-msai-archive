## The idea

A bridge between the Ethics and NLP threads: take a publicly available sentiment-analysis model and
test whether its predictions change when the only thing that changes in a sentence is a name or a
demographic term. The output is not a verdict on any vendor — it's practice at running a structured
fairness probe and documenting it without overclaiming.

## Approach

- Generate matched sentence pairs that differ by a single demographic signal, written by me
- Score both halves with a public model and measure the gap in predicted sentiment
- Summarize the result as a short model card with explicit limitations

## Why it's portfolio-safe

Everything here is public or self-authored: public models, synthetic test sentences I write myself, no
private or course data. It demonstrates the workflow — measure, document, qualify — rather than making
strong claims from a small sample. This is a planned project; the repo will go up once the probe and
write-up are clean.
