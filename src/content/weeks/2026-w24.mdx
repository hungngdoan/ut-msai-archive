## What I did

Before touching any models, I wanted the math underneath them to feel solid. This week was linear
algebra and probability review: the parts that show up constantly in deep learning rather than a
full re-study. I focused on eigen-decomposition and the singular value decomposition because they
keep reappearing in dimensionality reduction, optimization, and the geometry of what a layer does.

I also did the unglamorous logistics work: a rough cadence for how I want to split time across
Natural Language Processing, Ethics in AI, and Deep Learning once the term starts.

## What clicked

SVD finally stopped being a formula and started being a picture — any linear map as rotate, scale,
rotate. Holding that image makes a lot of downstream material easier to reason about.

## What didn't

Matrix calculus. I can follow a derivation when I read it, but producing one is still slow. That's a
real risk for Deep Learning, where I'll need to move through gradient derivations quickly.

## Plan for next week

- Drill matrix-calculus derivations until the common patterns are automatic
- Turn the probability cheat sheet into spaced-repetition cards
- Stand up the project repo and tooling so week one isn't spent on setup
