## What I did

The headline this week is infrastructure: I built the first version of this archive so that
tracking the program is low-friction once classes actually start. The bet is simple — if logging a
week takes two minutes and one file, I will keep doing it; if it takes a Saturday, I won't.

On the learning side, I went back to PyTorch basics rather than starting somewhere flashy. I
re-implemented a small multi-layer perceptron and, more importantly, worked the backward pass out on
paper before letting autograd do it. Matching the two by hand is the kind of thing that feels slow now
and pays off when a real network refuses to train in October.

## What clicked

Doing backprop by hand finally made the chain rule feel mechanical instead of mysterious. Once you
see that each layer just receives an upstream gradient and passes a transformed one backward, the
whole graph stops being intimidating.

## What didn't

Tensor broadcasting. I can make the shapes line up, but I'm pattern-matching, not reasoning. Next
week I want to be able to predict the output shape of an operation before I run it, every time.

## Plan for next week

- Write out the broadcasting rules in my own words and test myself on ten random shape pairs
- Read the optimization chapter of the Deep Learning book and take notes on Adam vs SGD
- Start the annotated transformer reading so I'm not cold on attention when NLP begins
